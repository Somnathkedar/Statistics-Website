What is Probability?

Probability is a branch of mathematics that deals with uncertainty. It helps us measure how likely an event is to happen.

In daily life, we often say things like:

  • There is a high chance of rain today
  • I might pass the exam
  • This medicine usually works

Probability converts these statements into numbers.

Range of Probability

Probability always lies between 0 and 1.

  • 0 means the event is impossible
  • 1 means the event is certain
  • Values between 0 and 1 show uncertainty

Basic Probability Formula

The basic formula of probability is:

Probability = Number of favourable outcomes Ã· Total number of outcomes

This formula is used when all outcomes are equally likely.

Real-Life Example: Tossing a Coin

When we toss a fair coin, there are two possible outcomes:

  • Head
  • Tail

Probability of getting a Head:

1 Ã· 2 = 0.5

This means there is a 50% chance of getting a Head.

Important Probability Terms

  • Experiment: An action with uncertain result (tossing a coin)
  • Outcome: A possible result (Head)
  • Sample Space: All possible outcomes
  • Event: One or more outcomes

Sample Space Explained

Sample space is the collection of all possible outcomes.

Example:

Tossing a die:

Sample space = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}

Types of Events

  • Impossible event: Probability = 0
  • Certain event: Probability = 1
  • Simple event: Single outcome
  • Compound event: More than one outcome

Real-Life Example: Exam Results

Suppose a student studies regularly. The probability of passing the exam increases.

Probability helps teachers and students understand performance trends.

Classical Probability

Classical probability is used when outcomes are equally likely.

Examples include:

  • Coins
  • Dice
  • Cards

Empirical (Experimental) Probability

Empirical probability is based on actual experiments or past data.

Example:

If it rained 20 days out of 100 days, then:

Probability of rain = 20 Ã· 100 = 0.2

Subjective Probability

Subjective probability is based on personal judgement or experience.

Example:

A doctor may say there is a high chance of recovery based on experience.

Probability Rules

  • Probability of an event is always between 0 and 1
  • Sum of probabilities of all outcomes = 1
  • Probability of impossible event = 0

Real-Life Example: Medical Testing

Probability is widely used in medical diagnosis.

Doctors use probability to understand:

  • Chance of disease
  • Accuracy of test results
  • Risk of complications

Probability Distributions

Probability distributions describe how probabilities are distributed across different outcomes.

Common distributions:

  • Binomial distribution
  • Normal distribution
  • Poisson distribution

Why Probability is Important

  • Used in statistics and data science
  • Helps in decision making
  • Used in weather forecasting
  • Important in medical research
  • Foundation of machine learning

Probability in Daily Life

Probability helps us understand risks and make better choices.

From deciding whether to carry an umbrella to choosing an investment, probability plays a silent but important role.

Conclusion

Probability is the language of uncertainty. It allows us to express unknown outcomes using numbers.

A strong understanding of probability builds the foundation for statistics, regression analysis, and data science.
