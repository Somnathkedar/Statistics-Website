What is Biostatistics?

Biostatistics applies statistical methods to biology, medicine, and public health research. It helps analyze medical data and supports evidence-based healthcare decisions.

Role of Biostatistics

Biostatisticians design experiments, analyze clinical data, evaluate treatments, and help improve patient outcomes.

Clinical Trials

Clinical trials test the safety and effectiveness of new drugs and treatments. Biostatisticians play a key role in study design and data analysis.

Career Opportunities

Popular roles include biostatistician, clinical data analyst, statistical programmer, and research analyst.

Q: Difference between descriptive and inferential statistics?
Descriptive statistics summarize data, while inferential statistics draw conclusions about a population using sample data.

Q: When is median preferred over mean?
Median is preferred when data are skewed or contain outliers.

Q: Standard deviation vs variance
Variance measures squared deviation; standard deviation is its square root.

Q: What is a p-value?
Probability of observing data assuming the null hypothesis is true.

Q: Type I vs Type II error
Type I: false positive. Type II: false negative.

Q: Phases of clinical trials
Phase I (safety), Phase II (efficacy), Phase III (confirmation), Phase IV (post-marketing).

Q: What is randomization?
Assigning subjects to groups by chance to reduce bias.

Q: Why is blinding important?
Prevents bias in treatment assessment.

Q: What is ITT analysis?
Includes all randomized subjects regardless of deviations.

Q: What is a primary endpoint?
The main outcome used to evaluate treatment effect.
