What is Biostatistics?

Biostatistics applies statistical methods to biology, medicine, and public health research. It helps analyze medical data and supports evidence-based decisions.

Role of Biostatistics

Biostatistics helps design experiments, analyze medical data, evaluate treatments, and improve healthcare outcomes.

Clinical Trials

Clinical trials test new drugs and treatments. Biostatisticians analyze trial data to ensure safety and effectiveness.

Career Opportunities

Biostatistics offers careers such as biostatistician, clinical data analyst, and research analyst.

Q: Difference between descriptive and inferential statistics?
Descriptive statistics summarize data using measures like mean, median, and graphs, while inferential statistics draw conclusions about a population using sample data.

Q: Mean, median, mode ‚Äì when is median preferred?
Median is preferred when data are skewed or contain outliers.

Q: Standard deviation vs variance
Variance measures average squared deviation, while standard deviation is its square root and easier to interpret.

Q: What is a p-value?
A p-value is the probability of observing the data assuming the null hypothesis is true.

Q: Type I and Type II error
Type I error is rejecting a true null hypothesis (false positive). Type II error is failing to reject a false null hypothesis (false negative).

2Ô∏è‚É£ Clinical Trials & Research Concepts

Q: Phases of clinical trials
Phase I assesses safety, Phase II evaluates efficacy, Phase III confirms effectiveness, and Phase IV monitors post-marketing safety.

Q: What is randomization?
Randomization assigns participants to treatment groups by chance to reduce bias.

Q: Why is blinding important?
Blinding prevents expectations from influencing results and reduces assessment bias.

Q: What is ITT analysis?
Intention-to-treat analysis includes all randomized subjects regardless of protocol deviations.

Q: What is a primary endpoint?
The primary endpoint answers the main research question of the study.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Statistical Tests & Models

Q: Linear vs logistic regression
Linear regression is used for continuous outcomes, while logistic regression is used for binary outcomes.

Q: Correlation vs regression
Correlation measures association, regression models relationships and predicts outcomes.

Q: Survival analysis
Survival analysis handles time-to-event data such as time to death or relapse.

Q: Kaplan‚ÄìMeier curve
A Kaplan‚ÄìMeier curve estimates survival probability over time.

Q: Hazard ratio
Hazard ratio compares the risk of an event between two groups.

4Ô∏è‚É£ SAS / R / Statistical Software

Q: DATA step vs PROC step in SAS
DATA step is used for data manipulation, PROC step is used for analysis.

Q: PROC REG vs PROC LOGISTIC
PROC REG performs linear regression, PROC LOGISTIC performs logistic regression.

Q: What are TLFs?
Tables, Listings, and Figures summarize clinical trial results.

Q: What is CDISC?
CDISC standards like SDTM and ADaM ensure standardized clinical trial data.

I use simple language, visuals, and focus on practical meaning.
