What is Statistics?

Statistics is the science of collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data.

In simple words, statistics helps us make sense of numbers. Without statistics, data is just a collection of meaningless values.

Why Do We Need Statistics?

In modern life, data is everywhere. Statistics helps us take correct decisions based on data instead of guesses.

  • Doctors use statistics to test medicines
  • Governments use statistics for planning
  • Businesses use statistics for profit analysis
  • Students use statistics to understand exam performance

Types of Statistics

Statistics is broadly divided into two types:

  • Descriptive Statistics
  • Inferential Statistics

Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics deals with summarizing and describing data. It does not make predictions.

It answers questions like:

  • What is the average?
  • How spread out is the data?
  • What is the most common value?

Inferential Statistics

Inferential statistics helps us draw conclusions about a population using a sample.

It answers questions like:

  • Can we generalize results?
  • Is the result statistically significant?
  • What is the margin of error?

Population and Sample

A population includes all individuals or items of interest. A sample is a subset taken from the population.

Example:

  • Population: All students in a college
  • Sample: 100 selected students

Why Do We Use Samples?

Studying the entire population is often impossible due to:

  • Time constraints
  • High cost
  • Practical limitations

Samples save time and money while still providing reliable information.

Types of Data

Data can be classified into:

  • Qualitative (Categorical) Data
  • Quantitative (Numerical) Data

Qualitative Data

Qualitative data represents categories or labels.

Examples:

  • Gender
  • Blood group
  • Marital status

Quantitative Data

Quantitative data represents numerical values.

  • Discrete Data: Whole numbers (number of children)
  • Continuous Data: Measurements (height, weight)

Levels of Measurement

  • Nominal
  • Ordinal
  • Interval
  • Ratio

These levels determine which statistical methods can be used.

Data Collection Methods

  • Surveys
  • Experiments
  • Observation
  • Secondary data sources

Data Presentation

Data can be presented using:

  • Tables
  • Bar charts
  • Pie charts
  • Histograms

Measures of Central Tendency

These measures describe the center of the data.

  • Mean
  • Median
  • Mode

Mean

Mean is the average of all values.

Median

Median is the middle value when data is arranged in order.

Median is useful when data has extreme values.

Mode

Mode is the most frequently occurring value.

Measures of Dispersion

Dispersion shows how spread out the data is.

  • Range
  • Variance
  • Standard deviation

Standard Deviation

Standard deviation measures how far values deviate from the mean.

A small standard deviation means values are close to the mean.
