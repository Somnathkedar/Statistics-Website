Regression Explained in Simple Language

With real-life examples and visuals

What is Regression?

Regression is a statistical method that helps us understand how one variable changes when another variable changes and allows us to make predictions.

Regression explains the relationship between an outcome variable and one or more predictor variables.

Real-Life Example: Age vs Blood Pressure

As age increases, blood pressure usually increases. Regression helps quantify this relationship.

Regression Equation

BP = β₀ + β₁ × Age
β₁ shows how much BP changes for each year increase in age.

Why Regression is Important

  • Used in medical research
  • Helps in prediction
  • Adjusts for confounding factors
  • Easy to interpret
